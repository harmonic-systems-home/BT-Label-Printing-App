Requirements

BTLabel works with the Brother P-touch PT‑P300BT label printer over Bluetooth, on macOS. It is designed for this specific printer and 12 mm tape.

Getting started

  1. Power on the PT-P300BT and load 12 mm tape.
  2. Open the Bluetooth menu in the macOS menu bar (or System Settings → Bluetooth) and connect to the PT-P300.
  3. If macOS prompts you to configure a printer, click Cancel — BTLabel talks to the printer directly and does not add it as a system printer.
  4. In BTLabel, click the Status refresh button next to the printer name. When connected, you'll see the loaded tape's width and color.

Frequently asked questions

It won't connect / Status shows nothing.

Re-check the Bluetooth connection in the menu bar — the printer must be paired and connected there first. Then click Status again. Make sure the printer is on and not connected to another device or app at the same time.

Which languages are supported?

Labels use Latin-script text (English and Western European languages such as French, German, Spanish, Italian, and Portuguese). Other scripts — Chinese, Japanese, Arabic, and so on — can be added by pasting an image into a label.

What's the difference between Basic and Advanced mode?

Basic mode (the default) keeps things simple: type your text and print. Turn on Advanced in the top bar to add multiple cells, symbols, images, and per-cell formatting.

How do substitution tokens work?

Type a token in a text cell and it fills in automatically: /n name, /p phone, /s street, /e email (set these in Settings), /i of /c for numbering, and /d (plus /d1–/d5) for dates. A token only applies when followed by a space, punctuation, or the end of a line.

Does my data sync between Macs?

Yes. Favorites, folders, history, and contact fields sync through your private iCloud account across Macs signed in to the same Apple ID. See our Privacy Policy for what is and isn't collected.

What does the purchase unlock?

BTLabel is free to try, with up to 5 free prints. A one-time in-app purchase unlocks unlimited printing. All design features are available during the trial.

Can I build it myself?

Yes — the full source is on GitHub and you may build it for your own use for free. The App Store version is a paid convenience (signed, notarized, auto-updating).
